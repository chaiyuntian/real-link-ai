# Short-Video Game Platform

This repo is no longer just a collection of isolated experiments. It is becoming a platform for turning short videos into many kinds of games.

## Platform idea

A short video is not only content to watch. It can become:

- a rhythm chart
- a branching story surface
- a puzzle timeline
- a surveillance feed
- a meme machine
- a companion rescue arc
- a causality test
- a director sandbox

The platform should let one clip generate many genres.

## Core loops

1. Discover a usable short video.
2. Understand it through human tagging, offline AI analysis, or both.
3. Insert gameplay points, segments, and narrative states.
4. Generate multiple playable interpretations from the same source.
5. Publish the games to one browsable platform.

## Platform sections

- `Play`
  - browse playable games by genre, clip, or mechanic

- `Create`
  - use authoring tools like Gameplay Point Studio

- `Analyze`
  - inspect offline AI understanding and annotation exports

- `Remix`
  - produce multiple game types from the same video

## Ten new game concepts

19. `Cutline Courier`
   - Deliver a message through a sequence of exact cut windows.

20. `Threat Weaver`
   - Rearrange danger points to change how the story is perceived.

21. `Companion Echo`
   - Use repeated appearances of a creature or ally as a memory-based mechanic.

22. `False Trailer`
   - Choose overlays and decisions that deliberately forge a different genre from the same clip.

23. `Momentum Trial`
   - Keep narrative momentum balanced across annotated segments or the plot collapses.

24. `Microloop Alchemist`
   - Combine tiny repeated fragments into spells, traps, or jokes.

25. `Scene Verdict`
   - Judge whether a scene is grief, duty, obsession, or hope based on evidence in the cut.

26. `Pursuit Cartographer`
   - Map the chase logic from segment data and survive only if your interpretation matches the route.

27. `Archive Diver`
   - Pull clues out of a short clip as if it were recovered archive footage.

28. `Myth Engine`
   - Push a grounded trailer toward legend by choosing symbolic reads at key frames.

## Why this direction matters

Short videos are cheap to source, fast to understand, and rich enough to support many game structures.

That makes them ideal for a platform where:

- one asset fans out into many playable products
- tooling compounds over time
- new games can be generated faster than new media needs to be found
